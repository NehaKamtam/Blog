Raigad’s story begins in the mists of time, where the rough terrain served as a natural defense, attracting settlers who sought refuge in its rocky landscape. Over the centuries, these early inhabitants carved out a livelihood, laying the foundations for what would eventually become a symbol of strength and resilience. However, it was during the reign of Shivaji Maharaj, the visionary Maratha warrior, that Raigad truly came into its own. Recognizing its strategic significance, Shivaji Maharaj transformed Raigad into a formidable fortress, perched majestically atop its rocky perch. From this vantage point, he orchestrated a campaign of resistance against the oppressive forces of the time, laying the groundwork for the Maratha Empire’s rise to prominence.
