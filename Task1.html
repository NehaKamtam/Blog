FORTS OF MAHARASHTRA

Maharashtra is the land of the great Maratha warrior Shivaji Maharaj, who once fought bravely against the mighty Mughal Empire. The valor of Maharashtra’s son Shivaji continues to echo in the strong royal forts nestled inside it.

Forts in Maharashtra were built during the times when India was under the foreign rule of the Mughals. Shivaji Maharaj brought the technique of guerrilla warfare and personally designed many of the Maharashtra forts.

Today, after centuries forts of Maharashtra have stood the test of time and weather, they stood the torrential rains, they stood blaze, and they stood dryness of weather along with the blood of enemies and the warriors likewise.

I am proud of the famous Maharashtra forts. In the list of forts in Maharashtra, many forts are won by Shivaji Maharaj. Some of these forts are, Raigarh, Murud Janjira, Sindhudurg, and Vijayadurg.

Each year millions of tourists visit Maharashtra’s strong forts built by Shivaji Maharaj or won by Shivaji Maharaj to witness the visual pieces of evidence of victories and history, not by Shivaji Maharaj alone but, those of the foreign rulers as well. Many of Maharashtra forts are built by Shivaji Maharaj to strengthen the kingdom of Maharashtra.


1.Raigad Fort

Raigad is a hill fort situated at about 25 Km from Mahad in the Raigad district. Chhatrapati Shivaji renovated this fort and made it his capital in 1674 AD. The rope-way facility is available at Raigad Fort, to reach at the fort from ground in few minutes. read more...

2.Shivneri Fort

Shivneri fort is birthplace of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. It is situated near Junnar town in the district of Pune, about 105 Kilometres from Pune. This fort is surrounded by steep upwards slopes from all four sides and is unassailable. There is small temple of Goddess Shivai and idols of Jijabai (Mother of Shri Shivaji) and Shivaji in his childhood. 3.Janjira Fort

Janjira is considered one of the strongest coastal forts in India. The fort is approached by sailboats from Rajapuri jetty. The main gate of the fort faces Rajapuri on the shore and can be seen only when one is about 40 feet (12 m) away from it. It has a small postern gate towards the open sea for escape.read more....
